OSV Autonomous Vehicle

ENES100 — "Urban Turtle" Over-Sand Vehicle

University of Maryland

Overview

The "Urban Turtle" was an autonomous Over-Sand Vehicle (OSV) built for the University of Maryland's ENES100 course. The project challenged teams to engineer a vehicle capable of navigating a sandy terrain course autonomously, performing specific tasks at designated waypoints.

Engineering Challenges

  • Sensor Integration: Designed and built sensor rigs combining multiple sensing modalities for terrain awareness and navigation.
  • Drivetrain Systems: Engineered drivetrain components capable of reliable locomotion over loose, uneven sand surfaces.
  • Autonomous Navigation: Implemented control algorithms enabling the vehicle to navigate between waypoints without human intervention.
  • Structural Design: Addressed challenges in making a robust yet lightweight chassis suitable for the competition environment.
  • System Integration: Coordinated mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems into a cohesive, functioning vehicle.
