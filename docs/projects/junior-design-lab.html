Junior Design Lab

Electrical Engineering — Two Semesters of Lab Coursework

Academic Coursework

Overview

Two semesters of Electrical Engineering Junior Lab coursework covering hands-on circuit design, simulation, fabrication, and testing. These labs developed core skills in analog and digital electronics, from transistor-level amplifier design to sequential logic circuits.

Operational Amplifier Design

Designed and simulated a multi-stage operational amplifier meeting strict specifications for voltage gain, input impedance, output impedance, and bandwidth. The design process involved iterating through transistor biasing, small-signal analysis, and frequency response optimization using circuit simulation tools like LTSpice.

Key specifications included achieving target open-loop gain, maintaining stability across the operating frequency range, and meeting power consumption constraints.

Prime Number Counter Circuit

Developed a synchronous digital counter implementing sequential logic to cycle through prime numbers. The circuit was designed using JK flip-flops, with combinational logic derived from Karnaugh maps and state transition diagrams.

This project exercised skills in state machine design, Boolean algebra simplification, and translating theoretical designs into working hardware on breadboards.
