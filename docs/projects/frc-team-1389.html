FRC Team 1389

FIRST Robotics Competition — "The Body Electric" — Software & Electrical

FIRST Robotics
2017–2018

Overview

I was a member of the software and electrical teams for FIRST Robotics Competition Team 1389, "The Body Electric," based at Walt Whitman High School. Over the 2017 and 2018 seasons, the team designed, built, and competed with robots featuring drop-six drivetrains, elevator mechanisms, cube intakes, and autonomous climbing systems. My work focused on robot software, autonomous routines, vision-assisted driving, and electrical system design and wiring.

Software Development

The robot software stack was written in Java using WPILib and deployed to a roboRIO controller. Key software contributions included:

  • Autonomous routines: Developed multiple autonomous programs including center gear and side gear placement routines. These used encoder-based path following and gyroscope-corrected heading to reliably score during the 15-second autonomous period.
  • Computer vision: Implemented vision pipelines for real-time target detection using on-board cameras, enabling automated alignment with field elements such as gear pegs and boiler targets. Camera feeds were also streamed to the driver station to aid manual driving.
  • Real-time web dashboards: Built WebSocket-based dashboards for live telemetry monitoring, giving the drive team and pit crew real-time insight into motor currents, sensor readings, and subsystem states during matches.
  • Reusable libraries: Created modular software libraries for common tasks (PID control, motion profiling, sensor abstraction) that were reused across seasons and shared with other teams.

Electrical Systems

As a member of the electrical team, I contributed to the design and assembly of the robot's electrical system, including power distribution, motor controller wiring, and sensor integration:

  • Power distribution: Wired the power distribution panel (PDP) to supply 12V battery power to all motors, the roboRIO, and auxiliary electronics with proper breaker sizing for each circuit.
  • Motor controllers: Configured and wired Talon SRX motor controllers for the drivetrain, elevator, and intake mechanisms, including CAN bus communication for closed-loop velocity and position control.
  • Sensor wiring: Integrated encoders, limit switches, gyroscopes, and camera modules into the electrical system, routing signal cables cleanly to minimize electromagnetic interference.
  • Cable management: Designed wiring harnesses and cable routing to withstand the impacts and vibrations of competition, ensuring reliability during multi-match days.

Competition Results

Team 1389 competed at multiple district events and the Chesapeake District Championship during the 2017 season:

  • Greater DC Regional (Week 2): Ranked 13th in qualifications. Selected for the 8th-seeded alliance, advanced to the finals and finished 2nd place. Validated the climber's reliability and identified gear intake improvements.
  • Central Maryland District (Week 4): Again ranked 13th in qualifications. Selected for the 5th-seeded alliance, advanced to the finals and won the event. Also awarded the Entrepreneurship Award.
  • CHS District Championship: Qualified for the championship based on accumulated district points from regional performance.
