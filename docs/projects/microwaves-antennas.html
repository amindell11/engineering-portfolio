Academic Coursework

Overview

Coursework in Microwaves and Antennas covering the theoretical foundations and practical laboratory work in RF engineering. The course covered microwave circuit theory, transmission line analysis, antenna fundamentals, and measurement techniques using vector network analyzers.

Topics Covered

  • Transmission Line Theory: Characteristic impedance, reflection coefficients, standing wave ratios, and Smith chart analysis.
  • S-Parameter Measurements: Two-port network characterization using scattering parameters, including calibration procedures and measurement uncertainty analysis.
  • Microwave Circuit Design: Impedance matching networks, power dividers, couplers, and filter design at microwave frequencies.
  • Antenna Fundamentals: Radiation patterns, gain, directivity, polarization, and antenna array theory.
  • Laboratory Skills: Hands-on experience with vector network analyzers, spectrum analyzers, and antenna measurement systems.
